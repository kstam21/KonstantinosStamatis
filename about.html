About Me

From soft actuators to dexterous robotic hands.

PhD candidate in Materials Science & Engineering at Texas A&M University, building the soft actuators and artificial muscles behind a next-generation robotic hand.

Professional Profile
Howdy! I'm Konstantinos.
PhD Candidate · Texas A&M NSF HAND ERC Researcher SPE President BS Valedictorian

I'm a Materials Science & Engineering PhD candidate at Texas A&M University. I've worked on anticancer metal complexes and magnetic materials — research that built my foundation in synthesis and characterization. Today, my main focus is soft actuators and artificial muscles for a next-generation robotic hand, combining hands-on lab work with AI-assisted research workflows to accelerate progress.

I care about connecting rigorous materials science to real-world engineering outcomes, with an eye toward commercializing the research I help develop.

Where the Work Goes

From lab bench to robotic hand.

We're building dexterous robotic hands that move with the strength and precision of real muscle. My contribution is the muscle itself: I design and synthesize LCE and PDES soft actuators.

I work closely with the center’s System Integration team to take these actuators from the lab bench into working hardware like the DexNex testbed shown here, refining each material for durability, force output, and compatibility with the rest of the hand.
