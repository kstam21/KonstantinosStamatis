Soft Actuator Development

Soft actuators that move like muscle.

I design two types of soft actuators — LCE and PDES — built to integrate directly into a dexterous robotic hand. Joule heating and electrostatic actuation are the two main mechanisms behind their muscle-like motion, enabling a next generation of intelligent robotic manipulation.

My Research Contribution · Aug 2023 – Present · Texas A&M University

Development of LCE & PDES Soft Actuators for Dexterous Robotic Hands

Under advisors Dr. Taylor Ware and Dr. Svetlana Sukhishvili (MSE & Biomedical Engineering, Texas A&M), and in collaboration with Dr. Ryan Truby at Northwestern University, I design LCE and PDES actuators for a dexterous robotic hand, driven by Joule heating through embedded conductive materials.

LCE ActuatorsDeveloping mechanically robust LCEs, leveraging dynamic covalent chemistry for tunable, recyclable actuation.
PDES ActuatorsDeveloping high-performance PDES actuators, leveraging chemistry for compliant, low-hysteresis artificial muscle fibers.
Thermomechanical CharacterizationActuation stress, strain, cycle life, and frequency response.
Structural AnalysisMicrostructure and morphology optimization.
Recyclability EngineeringDynamic covalent chemistry enables reprocessing.
