NSF HAND Engineering Research Center

Working across a national research network.

I conduct research within the NSF HAND ERC (Human AugmentatioN via Dexterity), a $52M, 10-year initiative headquartered at Northwestern University, developing dexterous, AI-powered robot hands that make robotic manipulation practical for real-world work.

Human AugmentatioN via Dexterity — HAND ERC

I am a PhD researcher within HAND ERC's Thrust 1 (Hands), based at Texas A&M University — one of the center's three core academic partners. My role focuses on cross-laboratory collaboration to design soft actuator materials for integration into the AI-driven robotic hand, alongside driving commercialization efforts and mentoring undergraduate researchers.

HAND ERC Structure

Three Research Thrusts

Thrust 1 — My Focus

Hands: Sensing, Actuation & Design

Engineering breakthrough robotic hand hardware with soft, durable skins and advanced actuators, including my LCE and PDES soft actuator research.

  • Soft actuators & artificial muscles (LCE and PDES) — my focus
  • Muscle-like, Joule-heated actuation for robotic fingers
  • Multimodal tactile sensing skin
  • Durable, compliant robotic hand hardware
Thrust 2

Intelligent Dexterity

AI-powered dexterous skill library using machine learning, sim-to-real transfer, and sensor-based control — enabling robots to learn complex manipulation.

  • Reinforcement learning for dexterity
  • High-fidelity soft-body simulation
  • Sensor-based real-time control
  • Composable dexterous skill library
Thrust 3

Human Interface

Intuitive, low-code interfaces enabling trained workers to deploy dexterous robots immediately, without specialized programming knowledge.

  • Haptic & VR multimodal interfaces
  • Natural language robot programming
  • Social & legal impact studies
  • Worker-centered integration design
